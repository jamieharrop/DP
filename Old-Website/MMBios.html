Lizzie Wort - Performer

Lizzie Wort is an actress/clown/puppeteer/musician. She trained at Elmhurst Ballet School, Bretton Hall, in Puppetry at Little Angel Theatre and in Clown with John Wright. She received critical acclaim in the solo show of Animal Farm, adapted and produced by Guy Masterson which toured worldwide for 2 years. She has worked with a variety of companies including National Theatre of Scotland, RSC, Little Angel Theatre, Jackson’s Lane Theatre, Ipswich New Wolsey, BAC, Soho Theatre, York Theatre Royal, Sevenoaks Playhouse, Tutti Frutti, Warhorse Theatre, Teasel Theatre, TongueTied Theatre, Tender Trap and Trinity Theatre and has appeared in several TV comedy sketches for BBC2, BBC3, CBBC & Channel 4. Recent theatre credits are a new clown version of Sweeney Todd with Finger In The Pie, an instrumental clown show To Have And To Honk directed by John Wright (Told By An Idiot) for The Honk Project and The Magician’s Daughter written by children's author Michael Rosen for Little Angel Puppet Theatre/RSC. Lizzie is co-founder of Galopin and co-wrote, made puppets for and performed in The Chronicles Of Bitter & Twisted at Assembly (George Square Two) in Edinburgh in 2011 followed by a run at Arcola Theatre. She is delighted to be working with Dyad Productions.

Elton Townend Jones – Writer/Director/Producer

Elton is a co-founder of Dyad Productions and, with Rebecca Vaughan, produced all five of their productions which have toured the UK and internationally. He wrote and directed his first play at the age of 18, whilst studying Performing Arts in East Anglia. First professional role: ‘Salieri’ in Cockpit Theatre’s Amadeus. Other roles include: ‘Puck’ (A Midsummer Nights Dream), ‘Herr Von Cuckoo’ (The Gingerbread Man), and ‘Friar Lawrence’ (Romeo and Juliet). TV appearances include: Lovejoy, The Chief and No Bananas. He has also directed professional tours of Shakespeare, and Teechers by John Godber. As a freelance journalist, he has written for Music-Zine and Kasterborous. His film column ‘Of Flickering Silver...’ is a central feature in Drunken Werewolf Magazine. He blogs as 1980s teenager Ritcherd Winterfood at www.25yearstoolate.blogspot.com He wrote, directed and appeared in the short film ‘Dear Eve…’ (2001), and wrote and developed Cutting The Cord for Flying Eye at the BAC (2009). In 2011 he wrote and performed in The Diaries Of Adam and Eve (published by StageScripts, 2013) and in 2012 he script-edited the critically acclaimed Female Gothic. He is also a professional voice artist.

Martin Tucker – Consultant Technician

Martin became interested in Technical Theatre while studying Performing Arts at West Herts College, supporting his technical studies as a casual technician at Dacorum Pavillion. He then studied Music Theatre at the University of Central Lancashire. He subsequently worked at The Wolverhampton Arena, the Maltings Arts Theatre, St Albans and The Gordon Craig Theatre, Stevenage. He has been head technician at The Old Town Hall, Hemel Hempstead for the last 3 years.

Waen Shepherd – Sound Designer

Waen is an actor, comedian and composer best known for his work as New Romantic musician Gary Le Strange. He has appeared in Hyperdrive (BBC2), The Inbetweeners (E4) and Lucy Montgomery’s Variety Pack (BBC Radio 4) and has created musical sounds for innumerable radio, TV and stage shows including Danny Robins’ Music Therapy (BBC Radio 4), The Wall (BBC3) and I, Elizabeth, The Diaries of Adam and Eve and Female Gothic (Dyad Productions).

Ben Guest – Photographer

Ben’s passion for imagery has inspired him to work as a professional advertising and commercial photographer since 1995, specialising in studio still life, room sets, exteriors and interiors on location, people and travel. Clients include well-known brand names B&B Italia, Sony, Debenhams, Harrods, BMW, Dixons, Littlewoods, Sotheby’s and Christies. He loves nothing more than composing and using light to make imagery something very special. View his portfolio or contact him at: www.benguest.com

Rebecca Vaughan – Producer

Rebecca trained at ALRA following a degree in English Literature at Cardiff University. Following a variety of theatre, television and film work, and feeling the need to be creative from the ground up, she founded Dyad Productions with Elton Townend Jones in 2009. Together, they have created and produced Austen’s Women, I, Elizabeth, The Diaries of Adam and Eve and Female Gothic, all of which premiered at the Edinburgh Festival and continue to tour throughout the UK and beyond.
