Rebecca Vaughan – Performer

Rebecca trained at ALRA following a degree in English Literature at Cardiff University. Film and television work includes the lead in Fearnort's feature film Grace's Land, the receptionist in The Interview and Jane Austen in The Dead, both for the BBC, plus an array of short films. For Dyad Productions, theatre credits include Female Gothic (Edinburgh 2013 & National Tour) The Diaries of Adam and Eve (Edinburgh 2012 and National Tour), I, Elizabeth (Edinburgh 2010, Adelaide 2011 & national tour), Austen's Women (Edinburgh 2009 and 2013, Adelaide 2010 and national tour). Other theatre credits include The Picture of Dorian Gray (Bill Kenwright, No 1 tour), Room 24 (Nuffield Theatre); Havisham (BAC). As associate artist with Lanternfish Theatre Company she played Hermia (A Midsummer Night's Dream) and Mrs Hawkins (Treasure Island). She studied physical theatre with Sachi Kimura (student of Philippe Gaulier) and continues her wider training at both The Actors Centre and City Lit, and can occasionally be found on the South Bank dabbling in a bit of Street Clowning.

Elton Townend Jones – Adapter and Director

Elton is a co-founder of Dyad Productions and, with Rebecca Vaughan, produced all five of their productions which have toured the UK and internationally. He wrote and directed his first play at the age of 18, whilst studying Performing Arts in East Anglia. First professional role: ‘Salieri’ in Cockpit Theatre’s Amadeus. Other roles include: ‘Puck’ (A Midsummer Nights Dream), ‘Herr Von Cuckoo’ (The Gingerbread Man), and ‘Friar Lawrence’ (Romeo and Juliet). TV appearances include: Lovejoy, The Chief and No Bananas. He has also directed professional tours of Shakespeare, and Teechers by John Godber. As a freelance journalist, he has written for Music-Zine and Kasterborous. His film column ‘Of Flickering Silver...’ is a central feature in Drunken Werewolf Magazine. He blogs as 1980s teenager Ritcherd Winterfood at www.25yearstoolate.blogspot.com He wrote, directed and appeared in the short film ‘Dear Eve…’ (2001), and wrote and developed Cutting The Cord for Flying Eye at the BAC (2009). In 2011 he wrote and performed in The Diaries Of Adam and Eve (published by StageScripts, 2013) and in 2012 he script-edited the critically acclaimed Female Gothic. He is also a professional voice artist.

Martin Tucker – Lighting Designer and consultant Technician

Martin became interested in Technical Theatre while studying Performing Arts at West Herts College, supporting his technical studies as a casual technician at Dacorum Pavillion. He then studied Music Theatre at the University of Central Lancashire. He subsequently worked at The Wolverhampton Arena, the Maltings Arts Theatre, St Albans and The Gordon Craig Theatre, Stevenage. He has been head technician at The Old Town Hall, Hemel Hempstead for the last 3 years.

Danny Bright – Sound Designer

Danny Bright is a sound designer, composer, recordist, musician and sonic manipulator working across the fields of theatre, performance, installation, music and media. He has lived, studied and worked in Sheffield, England; Burlington, Vermont, USA; and Brighton and Hove, England where he is now based. Recent credits include: Mata Hari: Female Spy (UK tour), Bond (UK, US, Australia tour), Nelson: The Sailor’s Story (The Forge, Adelaide), Woyzeck (UK tour & Redbridge Drama Centre), Inside the Memory Palace (MOSI), and Danny is a regular collaborator with Gavin Robertson, Scene Productions and Simply Told. His work has appeared at the New York City Electroacoustic Music Festival; World Soundscape Conference; Victoria & Albert Museum; Prague Quadrennial; Summerhall Edinburgh; British Science Festival; Manchester, Newcastle & Brighton Science Festivals; Edinburgh, Bedford & Brighton Fringe Festivals; MAGNA Science Adventure Centre; and toured the UK, Europe, Australia and the USA. Supporting organisations & commissions include Arts Council England, Octopus Collective, MAGNA Trust, and the Arts & Humanities Research Council as funder of his ongoing doctoral research in Music at the University of Sussex.

Ben Guest – Photographer

Ben’s passion for imagery has inspired him to work as a professional advertising and commercial photographer since 1995, specialising in studio still life, room sets, exteriors and interiors on location, people and travel. Clients include well-known brand names B&B Italia, Sony, Debenhams, Harrods, BMW, Dixons, Littlewoods, Sotheby’s and Christies. He loves nothing more than composing and using light to make imagery something very special. View his portfolio or contact him at: www.benguest.com
