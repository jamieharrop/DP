Rebecca Vaughan - Performer / Adaptor / Producer

Rebecca trained at ALRA following a degree in English Literature at Cardiff University. Film and television work includes the lead in Fearnort's feature film Grace's Land, the receptionist in The Interview and Jane Austen in The Dead, both for the BBC, plus an array of short films. Theatre credits include I, Elizabeth (Edinburgh 2010, Adelaide 2011 & national tour), Austen's Women (Edinburgh 2009, Adelaide 2010 & national tour), The Diaries of Adam and Eve (Edinburgh 2011 & national tour); The Picture of Dorian Gray (Bill Kenwright, No 1 tour), Room 24 (Nuffield Theatre); Havisham (BAC). As associate artist with Lanternfish Theatre Company she played Hermia (A Midsummer Night's Dream) and Mrs Hawkins (Treasure Island). She studied physical theatre with Sachi Kimura (student of Philippe Gaulier) and continues her wider training at both The Actors Centre and City Lit, and can occasionally be found on the South Bank dabbling in a bit of Street Clowning. She lives in St Albans with one husband and no cats.

Guy Masterson – Director

Born Guy Mastroianni. Education: Christ's Hospital School, Horsham; University of Wales, Cardiff (Biochem/Chem); UCLA school of Drama; LAMDA. First solo performance: The Boy's Own Story (1991); thence Under Milk Wood (1994). First Edinburgh (1994): Under Milk Wood & Playing Burton (director). The following 16 seasons saw him associated with some of Edinburgh's most celebrated hits: 12 Angry Men, One Flew Over The Cuckoo's Nest and The Odd Couple. As a performer, he won "The Stage Award Best Actor" for Fern Hill (2001) and was nominated for A Soldier's Song (1998) and Under Milk Wood (2003). As a director/producer, he has received the prestigious "Jack Tinker Spirit of the Fringe Award" (2003), eight "Scotsman Fringe First" and three "Herald Angels".

His production of Morecambe won the Olivier Award for Best Entertainment. Directorial credits include, I, Elizabeth, Long Live The King (2010); Morecambe, Austen's Women (2009); Reasonable Doubt, Scaramouche Jones (2008); The Eagle Dances, Follow Me, The Mistress (2007); Levelland (UK & OZ), Cooking With Puccini (2006); The Odd Couple, Swift (2005); Borge Again! 12 Angry Men - Oz (2004); 12 Angry Men - UK (2003); Goering's Defence, A Slight Tilt To The Left (2002); Resolution, Mom I'm Not A Lawyer (2001); All Words For Sex (2000); Adolf (1999); Hollywood Screams II (1998) Bye Bye Blackbird (1997); The House Of Correction (1996); and Playing Burton (UK 1994, NZ 2002). He is married to Brigitta and father to Indigo and Tallulah...

Elton Townend Jones – Script Editor / Producer / Voice Artist

As an actor and playwright, his most recent work was The Diaries of Adam and Eve (published in 2012) in which he played 'Adam'. He wrote and directed his first play whilst studying Performing Arts in East Anglia, aged 18. Since then, notable works include Cutting the Cord which he wrote and developed with Flying Eye Productions (BAC, 2009) and the film ‘Dear Eve…' (2001) which he wrote, directed and appeared in. Acting roles include: ‘Salieri' (Cockpit Theatre's Amadeus), Puck (A Midsummer Night's Dream), Herr Von Cuckoo (The Gingerbread Man), Friar Lawrence (Romeo and Juliet). TV appearances: Lovejoy, The Chief and No Bananas. He has directed professional tours of Shakespeare, and Teechers by John Godber. A professional journalist, he writes about music, film, theatre and TV for Drunken Werewolf Magazine and Kasterborous.com and blogs as 1980s teenager Ritcherd Winterfood at www.25yearstoolate.blogspot.com He is also a professional voice artist.

Kate Flanaghan – Costume Designer and Maker

Kate graduated from the Central School of Speech and Drama in 2007 with a B.A. (Hons) in Costume Construction. For Dyad Productions,: Costume Designer and Maker for I, Elizabeth (Edinburgh 2010, Adelaide 2011 & tour), Austen's Women (Edinburgh 2009, Adelaide 2010 & tour) and The Diaries of Adam and Eve (Edinburgh 2011 & tour); Other credits include Costume Maker for Julius Caesar (RSC); Costume Supervisor and Maker for the nationwide tour of dance production Bollywood Steps (Nutkhut) and an aerial performance by Compagnie Les Passagers for the finale of the 2007 Trafalgar Square Festival (Greenwich and Docklands Festivals), Costume Maker for an off-Broadway production of Woyzeck (Gate Theatre Productions) and Head of Wardrobe for Garsington Opera at Wormsley in 2012. Kate is a costume maker and wardrobe mistress for Evolution Productions, and travelling wardrobe mistress for Headliners Theatre Company. kflanaghan@btopenworld.com

Waen Shepherd – Sound Designer

Waen is an actor, comedian and composer best known for his work as New Romantic musician Gary Le Strange. He has appeared in Hyperdrive (BBC2), The Inbetweeners (E4) and Lucy Montgomery's Variety Pack (BBC Radio 4) and has created musical sounds for innumerable radio, TV and stage shows including Danny Robins' Music Therapy (BBC Radio 4), The Wall (BBC3) and I, Elizabeth and The Diaries of Adam and Eve both for Dyad Productions.

Ben Guest – Photographer

Ben's passion for imagery has inspired him to work as a professional advertising and commercial photographer since 1995, specialising in studio still life, room sets, exteriors and interiors on location, people and travel. Clients include well- known brand names B&B Italia, Sony, Debenhams, Harrods, BMW, Dixons, Littlewoods, Sotheby's and Christies.
